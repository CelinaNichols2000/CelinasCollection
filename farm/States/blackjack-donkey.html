The Last Chip

Your last chip did not even make a satisfying sound. It rolled to Vesper, kissed her nail, and became the price tag on your new collar.

Now every bray reminds you of the table laughing while your clothes tore away and Vesper mounted your back like the house had just won a saddle with ears.

She keeps replaying the hand out loud while brushing your mane: how you doubled down, how your face changed when the card ruined you, how your knees hit the floor before the curse had even finished deciding what kind of beast you deserved to be.

The worst part is how proud your body gets when she calls you useful. Your tail flicks, your ears twitch, and that empty donkey heat crawls up your spine while Vesper tells new players that you used to talk so confidently.
