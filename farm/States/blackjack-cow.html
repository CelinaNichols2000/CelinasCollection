Tagged By The House

The chip stopped spinning. Vesper pressed a brass tag to your collar, and your body answered like livestock paperwork had always been stronger than your will.

She led you away heavy, exposed, and mooing for the table that watched your last bits of pride get milked out with your debt.

Your clothes are still somewhere near the chair, split and useless. Vesper said she might frame them beside the shoe as proof that even a cocky little gambler can become farm profit when the house squeezes hard enough.

She likes to tap your tag before handling you, reading the number as if it is your real name now. Each little clink makes your cheeks burn and your body soften, heavy and obedient, waiting for her hands to decide what you are worth today.
