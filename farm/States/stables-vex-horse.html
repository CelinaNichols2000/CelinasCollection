Your Life in the Stall

You're in the head stall. The one with the good hay, the brass nameplate, and the crop hung on the post outside. Vex runs a very organized stable.

The change is setting in. Your posture is different. Your thoughts are slower and warmer.

#1 — THE ASSESSMENT
Vex: "Stand up straight. No, straighter. I'm checking your form." Her riding crop taps your shoulder blade. You don't understand why you straighten. You just do. She walks around you slowly. "Good bone structure. You'll carry well." The word 'carry' should worry you. It doesn't. Not anymore.
#2 — THE TACK
She puts the bridle on you like she's done it ten thousand times and you are the ten thousand and first. No ceremony. Just efficiency. The leather is cold then warm. The bit settles and your jaw works around it and the sound you make is not what you intended. "There. Now you look like what you are."
#3 — THE STALL
The stall door closes with a clean, final click. Fresh hay. The smell of it is complex and deeply correct. You lower your head and breathe it in before you decide to. Vex, outside the door: "Good. You've found the hay. Tomorrow we start ground work." You don't know what ground work means. Your body does.
#4 — RIDDEN
She's on your back and it's correct. It's the most correct thing that has happened. Her weight is distributed perfectly — she knows exactly how to sit. The reins move and your body responds before you register the input. "Good mare." Two words. Your whole chest fills with warmth. Your name — your old name — is very quiet.
#5 — OWNED
The brass plate on the stall door has a name on it. It's yours now. Not the old one — this one, the one Vex gave you the second day, said quietly and matter-of-factly while checking your hooves. You heard it and your ears turned. They do that now. They know your name. "You're my best horse," she says, and you believe her, and the stall feels like home.
