Pulled From Your Clothes

The table grew huge as the curse shrank you down. Vesper dug through your fallen clothes like she was looking for spare change and found you instead.

Now you are soft, twitching, and carried by the scruff whenever she wants to show off the lucky thing that used to think it could play cards.

She keeps your old lucky chip beside the tray, polished and useless. Every time someone asks about it, Vesper lifts you up and explains that luck did not save its owner from becoming something small enough to fit in her palm.

Your ears react to everything: the snap of cards, the clink of chips, the sweet cruel sound of her voice. Vesper calls it adorable. You call it humiliating, at least until your thoughts scatter into soft frightened little hops.
