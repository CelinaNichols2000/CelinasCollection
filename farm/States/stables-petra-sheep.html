Your Life Here

She's holding your hands. The fleece is starting in your palms. It's warm.

#1 — HANDS
She held both your hands, one palm in each of hers. Bilateral. "You'll feel it start in your hands. Just warmth first." Warmth. Then thickening. Then the texture of your palms shifting, going dense and soft.
#2 — FLEECE
It came in from your palms outward. White and thick and warm, spreading up your wrists and forearms. She kept holding your hands through all of it. "The fleece is coming in really beautifully," she said. "Pure white."
#3 — BLEAT
You bleated. Small and surprised. The sound just came. "Oh," she said, and she pulled you close and held you, and the warmth of her was the warmth of the wool and the warmth of the change all at once.
#4 — THE PEN
She carried you to the pen. Fresh hay, warm, small. The wool is yours. The pen is yours. "I'll bring the others tomorrow. Tonight is yours." She strokes your back — long, even strokes — and your whole body knows this is correct.
#5 — MORNING
