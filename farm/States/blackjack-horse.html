Ridden Out

Vesper caught your final chip under her heel and turned the shadow of her shoe into a bridle. You learned quickly that the house does not call a carriage.

She mounted you between the chairs and rode you slowly past every player who had watched you lose. Your new hooves clicked out the rhythm of your humiliation.

She still wears the same smile when she climbs onto your back: calm, polished, utterly certain you will move when she asks. You hate how quickly your body learned the pressure of her thighs and the little command hidden in each tap of her heel.

The table remembers you as the mount. Not the player, not the challenger, not the almost-winner. Just Vesper's gorgeous losing ride, bridled and displayed whenever the house wants to show what a bad bet looks like with four legs.
