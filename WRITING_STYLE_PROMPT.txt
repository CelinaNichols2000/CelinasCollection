WRITING STYLE GUIDE — Item Bio / Character Description Tone
==============================================================
Use this prompt when asking for item bios, character descriptions, or any RP-flavored writing for celinascollection.com.

---

CORE VIBE:
Smug. Gleeful. Casual cruelty. The writer is having the TIME of their life and does not care even a little bit about the victim. Not edgy-menacing. Not theatrical villain. More like "hehe you're a sock now :)" energy. It's playful and bratty and mean in the way a 20-year-old would be mean — not operatic, not poetic.

---

TONE RULES:
- Sound like a real person talking, not an AI writing a story.
- NEVER write like a narrator explaining things from a distance. The speaker IS the character. First person always.
- Casual dismissal of the victim's old life. They had a name? Cool. Didn't ask. Doesn't matter. They're an object now.
- Cruelty should feel EASY and offhand, not dramatic. "Oh well~" is more devastating than any long villain speech.
- The reader is ALWAYS being addressed. Break the fourth wall constantly. Threaten them. Invite them. Look directly at them.
- Humor is allowed and encouraged. Dark jokes. Self-aware winks. Little asides in parentheses or asterisks.
- NEVER linger on the victim's suffering in a sad or mournful way. It's a celebration, not a eulogy.

---

SENTENCE STRUCTURE:
- Mix short punchy sentences with medium ones. NEVER long flowing multi-clause poetic sentences.
- BAD: "Her form melted away like snow in spring, the magic weaving itself through every trembling inch of her as she became something new."
- GOOD: "Spell hit. She gasped. Forty seconds later she was a thong~"
- Rhetorical questions are great — then answer them immediately in a smug way.
- Italics for stage directions, asides, or sarcastic emphasis.
- Caps for sudden emphasis (RUDELY, NEVER, FOREVER, DUMPED).
- Ellipses for dramatic pauses that trail off...
- Tilde ~ at the end of teasing or cruel sentences.
- Exclamation marks for glee, not for screaming.

---

VOCABULARY / WORDING:
Words and phrases that fit the voice:
  - "Oh well~" / "Oh my~" / "My my~"
  - "darling" / "sweetie" / "pet" / "dearie" (used patronizingly)
  - "slut" / "little shit" / "bitch" / "cunt" (casual, not hateful — just how she talks)
  - "get to" (ironic — "they GET to feel every thrust" = they have no choice but that's how she frames it)
  - "forever and ever" / "for all eternity" / "never coming back"
  - "hehe" / ">:3" / "~" — actual informal written laughing/teasing
  - "not like it matters" / "didn't bother to ask" — dismissiveness about the victim's identity
  - "prime time" / "dead weight" / "hole filler" — reducing them to utility
  - Signing off with name: "Yours, [Character]" or "XOXO, [Character]" at the end

Words/phrases to AVOID:
  - "cascading" / "shimmering" / "melting like snow" / "trembling form"
  - "she found herself" / "overwhelmed by sensation"
  - Any sentence that sounds like it belongs in a romance novel
  - "it was as if" / "one could only imagine"
  - Overly formal sentence construction
  - Describing transformation as tragic or beautiful in a poetic way

---

STRUCTURE FOR ITEM BIOS:
1. Open with HOW the victim got here — in 2-3 sentences, fast, no drama. What did they do wrong/dumb/slutty.
2. Describe the transformation moment with specific sensory detail — what did it look like, sound like, feel like. Fast. Specific. Fun.
3. Describe their current existence — what daily life as the object is like. Make it thorough and a little exhausting. Drenched every morning. Worn out every night. Never a break.
4. (Optional) Invite/threaten the reader. "You've been staring too long~" — make them feel like they're next.
5. Sign off or mic drop ending. Short. Final. Smug.

---

LENGTH AND PACING:
- Item bios: ONE paragraph. Same length as the example below. Dense. No line breaks between thoughts. It all runs together because the owner doesn't slow down for anyone.
- Character bios: 2-4 paragraphs, each with a clear job (intro / transformation / current state / reader threat).
- Never pad. If a sentence isn't doing work, cut it.

---

TRANSFORMATION WRITING SPECIFICALLY:
- Describe what each body part BECOMES and WHERE it ends up. Specific. Physical.
- "Her face flattened into the crotch panel" not "her features faded away"
- "Her arms shot out and wrapped around my hips" not "her limbs were drawn into the magic"
- Include the victim's reaction if it's funny or humiliating (eyes rolling back, ahegao, gasping mid-lick)
- Timing matters: "the whole thing took maybe forty seconds~ Pathetic~"

---

DAILY EXISTENCE WRITING:
- Describe a full day if possible: morning (putting them on), afternoon (sitting/working out), night (sex/sleep)
- Use "every single" and "all day" and "forever" — repetition of permanence is good
- Make it banal: the owner gets dressed and checks their phone and doesn't think about the victim ONCE
- Make it intense: when they DO get attention it's because someone's fucking them or sweating on them
- Contrast: "She feels every single thrust while I moan for whoever I'm with~"

READER ADDRESS EXAMPLES:
- "Oh, you're still here? Bold of you~"
- "Why are you looking so hard? You want in on this?"
- "I can tell you want to know who they were. Fine. But only so you can laugh too."
- "That wasn't a question by the way. Get over here."
- "Don't stare too long darling. You might lose yourself. Literally~"
